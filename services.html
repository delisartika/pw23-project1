A facelift, also known as a rhytidectomy, is a cosmetic surgical procedure designed to reduce signs of aging on the face and neck. The goal is to tighten sagging skin, eliminate wrinkles, and create a younger, fresher appearance.

Before undergoing a facelift, the patient will consult with a plastic surgeon to assess the skin condition, aesthetic needs, and patient expectations. The doctor will explain the procedure in detail, provide information regarding risks and recovery, and plan the most appropriate technique.

There are several types of facelifts, including traditional facelifts, mini facelifts, and neck facelifts. The type of facelift chosen will depend on the patient's aesthetic needs and goals.
